Complete Calculator Guides

Age Calculator – Calculate Your Exact Age

Our free age calculator tells you your exact age in years, months, and days from your date of birth. It also shows the total number of days you have lived. Simply enter your date of birth and today's date. Useful for filling government forms, passport applications, school admissions, and retirement planning. Example: Born on 1 January 1995 → Age on 14 March 2026 = 31 years, 2 months, 13 days.

BMI Calculator – Check Your Body Mass Index

The BMI calculator measures body fat based on your height and weight. Formula: BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height² (m). A BMI below 18.5 is underweight, 18.5–24.9 is normal, 25–29.9 is overweight, and above 30 is obese. Our calculator supports both metric (kg/cm) and imperial (lbs/inches) units and also shows your ideal healthy weight range. Recommended by doctors and fitness experts worldwide.

Loan EMI Calculator – Monthly Instalment for Any Loan

The EMI calculator instantly computes your monthly loan instalment for home loans, car loans, personal loans, and education loans. Formula: EMI = [P × r × (1+r)ⁿ] ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]. Example: ₹5,00,000 loan at 8.5% for 5 years = EMI of ₹10,254/month. Also shows total interest payable and total payment amount so you can plan your finances better.

Percentage Calculator – Fast % Calculations

Our percentage calculator solves three types of problems: (1) What is X% of Y? (2) X is what percent of Y? (3) Percentage change from X to Y. Examples: What is 15% of 200 = 30. 45 is what % of 180 = 25%. Change from 100 to 125 = +25%. Useful for calculating exam marks, discounts, tax, profit/loss, and salary hikes.

Date Difference Calculator – Days Between Two Dates

The date difference calculator finds the exact number of days, weeks, months, and years between any two dates. Useful for calculating project deadlines, loan tenure, age verification, contract duration, pregnancy weeks, and anniversary countdowns. Example: From 1 Jan 2020 to 14 Mar 2026 = 6 years, 2 months, 13 days = 2,265 total days.

GST Calculator – Add or Remove GST Instantly

India's GST calculator helps you add or remove Goods & Services Tax from any amount. Supports all GST slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Also shows CGST and SGST split. Example: ₹1,000 + 18% GST = GST Amount ₹180, Total ₹1,180 (CGST ₹90 + SGST ₹90). Essential for business owners, shopkeepers, accountants, and freelancers filing GST returns.

SIP Calculator – Estimate Mutual Fund Returns

The SIP calculator estimates the future value of your Systematic Investment Plan using compound interest. Formula: M = P × [(1+r)ⁿ − 1] ÷ r × (1+r). Example: ₹5,000/month for 10 years at 12% annual return = Invested ₹6,00,000 → Maturity value ₹11,61,695 → Profit ₹5,61,695. Perfect for planning retirement, child education, and wealth creation goals.

Discount Calculator – Find Sale Price After Discount

The discount calculator tells you the final price after applying a discount percentage, or calculates what discount was given based on original and sale price. Example: Original price ₹1,500 with 20% discount = You save ₹300, Final price ₹1,200. Also works reverse: if MRP is ₹2,000 and sale price is ₹1,600 → Discount = 20%. Great for shopping, billing, and retail businesses.

Mortgage Calculator – Home Loan Payment Estimator

Our mortgage calculator helps you estimate monthly home loan EMI including principal and interest breakdown. Enter home price, down payment, interest rate, and loan tenure. Example: Home price ₹50,00,000, down payment ₹10,00,000, 8.5% rate, 20 years = Monthly EMI ₹34,825, Total interest ₹43,58,000. Helps first-time home buyers plan their budget before applying for a housing loan.

Time Calculator – Add, Subtract & Convert Time

The time calculator lets you add or subtract two time durations and convert between seconds, minutes, hours, and days. Example: 2 hours 45 minutes + 1 hour 30 minutes = 4 hours 15 minutes = 15,300 seconds. Useful for calculating work hours, billing time, travel duration, project timesheets, and overtime calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions

▸  How is EMI calculated for a home loan?

EMI is calculated using the formula: EMI = [P × r × (1+r)ⁿ] ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ − 1], where P is the loan principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly instalments. For example, a ₹30 lakh loan at 8.5% for 20 years gives a monthly EMI of approximately ₹26,035.

▸  What is a good BMI for Indians?

For Asian and Indian populations, health experts recommend a slightly lower BMI range. A BMI of 18.5–22.9 is considered normal, 23–24.9 is overweight, and 25 or above is obese. This is because Asians tend to have higher body fat percentage at lower BMI values compared to Western populations.

▸  What are the GST slabs in India?

India has four main GST slabs: 5% (essential goods like food, medicines), 12% (processed food, computers), 18% (most services, electronics, restaurants), and 28% (luxury goods, cars, tobacco). Some items like fresh vegetables, milk, and eggs are exempt from GST (0%).

▸  Is SIP better than FD for long-term investment?

For long-term goals (5+ years), SIP in equity mutual funds historically delivers 10–15% annual returns vs FD's 6–7%. However, SIP carries market risk while FD is guaranteed. For goals over 10 years like retirement or child education, SIP is generally considered better due to the power of compounding and inflation-beating returns.
